# DataDiet Research & Evidence Base

This document outlines the scientific research and health guidelines that inform DataDiet's tracking thresholds and recommendations.

## Microplastics Exposure

### Plastic Bottles
**Threshold:** >0.5/day moderate concern, >1/day elevated concern

**Research Findings:**
- Consuming tap water leads to ~4,000 microplastics (MPs) annually
- Bottled water consumption adds ~90,000 additional MPs per year
- No official Acceptable Daily Intake (ADI) exists for microplastics

**Health Concerns:**
- Microplastics can act as vectors for chemical additives (phthalates, bisphenols)
- Associated with oxidative stress, inflammatory responses, and immune dysregulation
- Smallest particles (<1 Âµm) can penetrate deep into lungs and potentially enter bloodstream

**Sources:**
- [Systematic review of microplastics intake via food (2024)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39431565/)
- [Microplastics in Drinking Water Review (PMC)](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12474263/)
- [UCSF: How to Limit Microplastics Dangers](https://www.ucsf.edu/news/2024/02/427161/how-to-limit-microplastics-dangers)

### Hot Food in Plastic
**Threshold:** Any regular exposure is concerning

**Research Findings:**
- Heat significantly increases plastic leaching
- Microplastic release accelerates with temperature
- Avoid microwaving food in plastic containers

## Processed Meat

**Threshold:** >2 servings/week moderate, >3 servings/week elevated

**Research Findings:**
- WHO/IARC classifies processed meat as **Group 1 carcinogen** (same category as tobacco)
- 18% increased colorectal cancer risk per 50g/day increase
- ~34,000 global cancer deaths/year attributed to processed meat
- World Cancer Research Fund recommends avoiding processed meat entirely

**What Counts as Processed Meat:**
- Bacon, sausage, hot dogs, ham, deli meats
- Salami, pepperoni, corned beef
- Smoked, cured, salted, or preserved meats

**Sources:**
- [WHO Q&A: Carcinogenicity of Red and Processed Meat](https://www.who.int/news-room/questions-and-answers/item/cancer-carcinogenicity-of-the-consumption-of-red-meat-and-processed-meat)
- [WCRF: Limit Red and Processed Meat](https://www.wcrf.org/research-policy/evidence-for-our-recommendations/limit-red-processed-meat/)
- [Harvard Nutrition Source: Understanding the Findings](https://nutritionsource.hsph.harvard.edu/2015/11/03/report-says-eating-processed-meat-is-carcinogenic-understanding-the-findings/)

## Ultra-Processed Foods

**Threshold:** >30% of meals moderate, >50% elevated

**Research Findings:**
- Average American consumes **55% of calories** from ultra-processed foods
- Each 100g/day increase associated with:
  - 2.6% higher all-cause mortality
  - 14.5% higher hypertension risk
  - 5.9% increased cardiovascular events
  - 19.5% higher digestive disease risk
- 10% increase in UPF consumption = 64% higher prediabetes risk in young adults

**Most Harmful Types (by mortality risk):**
1. Processed meat (highest risk)
2. Sugar-sweetened beverages
3. Artificially sweetened beverages
4. Dairy-based desserts
5. Ultra-processed breakfast foods

**Sources:**
- [CDC Data Brief: Ultra-Processed Food Consumption (2025)](https://www.cdc.gov/nchs/products/databriefs/db536.htm)
- [Umbrella Review: UPF and Health Outcomes (PubMed)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38418082/)
- [Johns Hopkins: UPF Account for Half of Calories](https://publichealth.jhu.edu/2024/ultraprocessed-foods-account-for-more-than-half-of-calories-consumed-at-home)
- [Harvard: Ultra-Processed Foods Linked to Early Death](https://hsph.harvard.edu/news/ultra-processed-foods-some-more-than-others-linked-to-early-death/)

## Caffeine

**Threshold:** >3/day moderate, >4/day elevated; any after 2pm flagged

**Research Findings:**
- FDA maximum: **400mg/day** (~4 cups of coffee)
- Toxic effects (seizures) at ~1,200mg rapid consumption
- Caffeine after 2pm significantly impacts sleep quality
- Pregnant women: limit to 200mg/day

**Standard Caffeine Content:**
- 8 oz brewed coffee: ~95mg
- 8 oz black tea: ~47mg
- 12 oz cola: ~34mg
- Energy drink (16 oz): ~160mg

**Sources:**
- [FDA: How Much Caffeine is Too Much?](https://www.fda.gov/consumers/consumer-updates/spilling-beans-how-much-caffeine-too-much)
- [Mayo Clinic: Caffeine Guidelines](https://www.mayoclinic.org/healthy-lifestyle/nutrition-and-healthy-eating/in-depth/caffeine/art-20045678)

## Alcohol

**Threshold:** >7 drinks/week moderate, >14 drinks/week elevated

**Research Findings:**
- CDC moderate drinking guidelines:
  - Women: 1 drink or less per day
  - Men: 2 drinks or less per day
- Heavy drinking defined as:
  - Women: 8+ drinks/week
  - Men: 15+ drinks/week
- Alcohol accounts for **6% of all US cancers** and 4% of cancer deaths
- Even moderate drinking increases mortality risk vs. not drinking

**Standard Drink Sizes:**
- 12 oz regular beer
- 5 oz wine
- 1.5 oz spirits (80 proof)

**Sources:**
- [CDC: About Moderate Alcohol Use](https://www.cdc.gov/alcohol/about-alcohol-use/moderate-alcohol-use.html)
- [NIAAA: Defining How Much is Too Much](https://www.niaaa.nih.gov/health-professionals-communities/core-resource-on-alcohol/basics-defining-how-much-alcohol-too-much)

## Sodium

**Threshold:** >0.7 high-sodium meals/day moderate, >1.2/day elevated

**Research Findings:**
- FDA Daily Value: **<2,300mg/day**
- AHA ideal limit: **<1,500mg/day** (especially for those with high blood pressure)
- Average American intake: **3,400mg/day** (48% over recommended)
- 70%+ of sodium comes from processed/packaged foods (not table salt)

**Health Impact:**
- Excess sodium raises blood pressure
- Major risk factor for heart disease and stroke
- Reducing 1,000mg/day can significantly improve heart health

**Sources:**
- [FDA: Sodium in Your Diet](https://www.fda.gov/food/nutrition-education-resources-materials/sodium-your-diet)
- [FDA: Sodium Reduction in Food Supply](https://www.fda.gov/food/nutrition-food-labeling-and-critical-foods/sodium-reduction-food-supply)
- [AHA: Why Should I Limit Sodium?](https://www.heart.org/-/media/files/health-topics/answers-by-heart/why-should-i-limit-sodium.pdf)

## Late Night Eating

**Threshold:** >15% of meals after 9pm moderate, >25% elevated

**Research Findings:**
- Late eating (after 9pm or within 3 hours of sleep) associated with:
  - **2-3x increased obesity risk**
  - 10% slower fat metabolism
  - Nocturnal glucose intolerance
  - Disrupted circadian rhythm
- Late-night eating delays melatonin onset and elevates cortisol
- Associated with metabolic syndrome, poor sleep, and mood disorders

**Recommendation:**
- Maintain 3-hour window between eating and bedtime
- Allows for digestion and blood sugar regulation

**Sources:**
- [PMC: Metabolic Effects of Late Dinner (Clinical Trial)](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7337187/)
- [UCLA Health: Eating Later May Lower Metabolism](https://www.uclahealth.org/news/article/research-suggests-eating-later-may-lower-metabolism)
- [PMC: Night Eating and Metabolic Syndrome](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC6288903/)

## Fried Foods

**Threshold:** >3/week moderate, >5/week elevated

**Research Findings:**
- Linked to increased cardiovascular disease risk
- Associated with inflammation and obesity
- Higher cardiovascular mortality with frequent consumption
- Healthier alternatives: baking, grilling, air frying

---

## Charred/Grilled Foods

**Threshold:** >2/week moderate, >4/week elevated

**Research Findings:**
- Charring creates:
  - **Heterocyclic amines (HCAs)** - form when meat is cooked at high temperatures
  - **Polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs)** - form when fat drips on flames
- Both HCAs and PAHs are associated with increased cancer risk
- Risk reduction: trim charred portions, marinate meat, avoid direct flame contact

## Sugary Beverages

**Threshold:** >0.5/day moderate, >1/day elevated

**Research Findings:**
- Highest mortality risk among all ultra-processed food categories
- Strong links to:
  - Weight gain and obesity
  - Type 2 diabetes
  - Metabolic syndrome
  - Cardiovascular disease

**Sources:**
- [Harvard: UPF and Mortality](https://hsph.harvard.edu/news/ultra-processed-foods-some-more-than-others-linked-to-early-death/)

## Individual Sensitivity Signals

### Spicy Foods (for gut sensitivity)
**Threshold:** >4/week moderate, >7/week elevated

- Track if you have IBS, IBD, or gut sensitivity
- Individual tolerance varies significantly
- May trigger discomfort in sensitive individuals

### Acidic Foods (for reflux)
**Threshold:** >4/week moderate, >7/week elevated

- Track if you have GERD or acid reflux
- Common triggers: tomatoes, citrus, vinegar-based foods
- Individual tolerance varies
